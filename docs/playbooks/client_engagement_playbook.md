## Purpose

This playbook is the operating sequence for a DefendableOS client engagement. It is the **MAGIC funnel** (Meetings · Appraisals · Ink · Close) applied to an AI defense relationship · with every stage producing a named artifact · a named owner · a named escalation trigger.

This is NOT a marketing funnel. This is a broker-grade relationship sequence · the same one that closed $8B in CRE · adapted to a 24/7 defense service.

Read this in full before any first dial. Re-read the stage you're currently operating in before any client touch.

## The MAGIC funnel · applied

```
M · Meetings
A · Appraisals
G · (G is silent · we don't ride hype · the silent gap is where the broker proves it)
I · Ink
C · Close
```

Five stages · one engagement. Skipping a stage breaks the relationship. Compressing a stage breaks the trust.

## Stage 1 · MEETINGS

**Goal**: Establish that there's a real pain · a real principal · and a real fit. Get to the sit-down.

**Owner**: Jr broker (the Swarm BD operators) · with sr broker available for principal-level deals.

**Cadence**: 5-8 touches over 14-21 days. Cold dial · warm intro · networked referral · LinkedIn DM · email · whatever the channel · the rule is humanized · NOT mass-blasted.

**What you produce**:
- A `lead-{slug}` folder with every touch logged
- A 1-page **discovery note** after the first real conversation (NOT a transcript · operator's interpretation)
- A go/no-go recommendation to sr broker on whether to escalate to Appraisals

**What you DON'T do**:
- Don't pitch features in stage 1. The first real conversation is 80% listening · 20% asking sharp questions. The principal does the talking. You take the color.
- Don't quote prices. Pricing belongs in the Letter of Understanding · not in a cold call.
- Don't promise capabilities. The PASS doctrine starts here · before you've even named our terms.

**Escalation triggers**:
- Principal mentions a regulator deadline → sr broker on the next call
- Principal mentions a competitor already in proposal → sr broker on the next call
- Three failed touches without response → drop the dial · re-queue in 90 days

**Stage exits**: principal agrees to a 30-minute discovery call AND identifies themselves as the decision-maker (NOT IT · NOT procurement · NOT "let me check with my team"). If they're not the principal · don't run the deal · ask for the principal warmly · or PASS.

## Stage 2 · APPRAISALS

**Goal**: Verify the pain is real · the asset is defensible · the engagement pencils on our side · the principal can pencil it on theirs.

**Owner**: Sr broker · with jr broker building the color file in parallel.

**Cadence**: 14-30 days of color-building + 1-3 working sessions with the principal.

**What you produce**:
- A **color file** per the color doctrine (≥ 5 independent sources · contradictions reconciled · timestamps fresh)
- A **pre-market flight sheet** per the pre-market flight sheet doctrine (the broker-grade internal doc · NOT the marketing OM)
- A **PASS verdict log** · explicit go/no-go from the sr broker · in writing · stored in the engagement folder
- A **draft proposal** with specific success criteria · numeric · time-bound · auditable

**What you DON'T do**:
- Don't skip the PASS check. Fantasy mandates kill assignments and burn referral pipeline. PASS is the discipline.
- Don't propose without the color file. A proposal without color is a sales pitch. We don't run sales pitches.
- Don't soften the success criteria. The criteria are the contract. Vague criteria produce vague closings.

**Escalation triggers**:
- Color file can't reach 5 sources after 14 days → sr broker pauses · re-tests assumptions
- PASS verdict comes in negative → founder is informed within 24 hours · we walk · we don't argue
- Principal asks for criteria changes that move the boundary > 25% → re-pencil from scratch · don't band-aid

**Stage exits**: PASS verdict positive · color file complete · principal agrees to review the formal LOU.

---

## Stage 3 · INK

**Goal**: Sign the Letter of Understanding. The LOU is the engagement contract · it names tier · fee structure · success criteria framework · escalation pyramid · cost-to-mint ceiling · SLA.

**Owner**: Sr broker (drafts) · founder (countersigns on T3/T4 white-glove or embedded tiers) · principal (signs).

**Cadence**: 7-14 days from delivered LOU to signed LOU. If it's taking longer · the engagement isn't real. Acknowledge that.

**What you produce**:
- A **Letter of Understanding** per the LOU example template (`docs/examples/sample_letter_of_understanding.md`)
- A **counter-signed engagement brief** for the first assignment under the LOU (Assignments are added under the LOU · they don't replace it)
- A **principal-facing kick-off doc**: who they call · how Morning Briefs arrive · what the dashboard looks like

**What you DON'T do**:
- Don't sign a vague LOU. The criteria · the fees · the SLA · the escalation pyramid · ALL of it must be specific.
- Don't accept verbal commitments. CRE doctrine: if it's not on paper · it didn't happen.
- Don't skip the principal walkthrough of the LOU. The principal must understand what they're buying. Reading is not enough · we WALK them through it.

**Escalation triggers**:
- Principal asks to remove the cost-to-mint ceiling → sr broker explains why we don't do that · if principal insists · PASS
- Principal asks to remove the Fix-or-Refund 90-day clause (T4 only) → red flag · founder reviews
- Principal asks to skip the kick-off walkthrough → red flag · they're treating this like a SaaS sub · we re-educate before signing

**Stage exits**: LOU signed by both sides · stored in books-and-records as a deed · `DDEED-{org}-LOU-{slug}-v1` · Hedera-anchored · ENS-resolvable.

## Stage 4 · (the silent G · the gap · the proof)

**Goal**: Between signing the LOU and the first material close · the broker proves the apparatus works. NOT a vibe stage · a discipline stage.

**Owner**: Defense ops team · with sr broker on principal touchpoints.

**Cadence**: First 30-90 days of in-flight operations. Pilot tier (T1) is explicitly this phase by name.

**What you produce**:
- Daily **Morning Briefs** to the principal at 06:00
- Nightly **reconciliation deeds** from the 2am Tribunal cron
- Weekly **sr broker review** · summarized in the Friday Morning Brief
- A **calibration report** at day 30 · 60 · 90 · comparing predicted Probability of Close vs actual Honey rate · proving the math

**What you DON'T do**:
- Don't go silent. The G stage is where broker discipline shows up. Silence breaks trust before any failure does.
- Don't over-promise improvements. We name what we see · we name what we'll do · we don't hype.
- Don't let the principal forget what they signed up for. The Morning Brief reminds them weekly of the success criteria · in their language.

**Escalation triggers**:
- Probability of Close drops two bands in a 7-day window → sr broker on a same-day call
- Any Propolis event on a critical path → founder informed within 24 hours
- Principal goes 14 days without acknowledging a Morning Brief → sr broker reaches out · check the relationship pulse

**Stage exits**: 90 days completed · first formal Defendable Closing Statement issued · principal either renews to Production tier (T2) · upgrades to White-Glove (T3) · or PASSes (mutual decision · no hard feelings · doors stay open).

---

## Stage 5 · CLOSE

**Goal**: Close the assignment with a formal Defendable Closing Statement · update the LOU for the next assignment · book the next dial.

**Owner**: Sr broker + QA validator (SH6) for the closing statement · founder for principal-level renewals.

**Cadence**: Closing statement issued within 5 business days of assignment completion. Next-assignment kick-off within 14 days.

**What you produce**:
- A **Defendable Closing Statement** per the closing statement template (`docs/examples/...`)
- The 5-grade breakdown · composite · boolean outcome · variance vs contract
- A `DDEED-{org}-CLOSE-{slug}-{seq}-v1` deed issued · anchored · resolved
- A **renewal proposal** OR a **graceful exit doc** depending on the outcome

**What you DON'T do**:
- Don't pad the boolean. CLOSED · HONEY means CLOSED · HONEY. CONDITIONED means CONDITIONED. FAILED means FAILED. The closing statement is signed truth.
- Don't pivot to upsell in the same conversation as a FAILED close. The principal needs the failure named · the recovery plan presented · and breathing room. Upsell follows trust · not the other way around.
- Don't forget the relationship. Even a graceful exit produces referrals. White-glove discipline doesn't stop at the close.

**Escalation triggers**:
- FAILED · DARK outcome → founder runs the close meeting
- Principal disputes the closing statement → sr broker walks the math · founder reviewable
- Renewal probability < 50% by day 7 post-close → relationship recovery protocol · founder optional

**Stage exits**: deed issued · folder filed · next-assignment booked OR engagement closed clean.

## White-glove · what it actually means

White-glove is not a marketing word. White-glove is a discipline. It means:

- The principal has a named sr broker. Not a queue. Not a chatbot. A person.
- The Morning Brief arrives at 06:00. Every day. Including the day after a holiday.
- The dial moves on the dashboard in real-time. Not "we'll update next week."
- The founder is reachable on text for T3 and T4 tiers. Not for everything. For the things that need the founder.
- Receipts are issued. Deeds are anchored. Books are filed. Always.
- We acknowledge wins AND failures by name. Both within 24 hours of detection.
- We walk the principal through every closing statement. We don't email a PDF and ghost.

White-glove is not soft. RAVE (Respect · Value · Appreciate Everyone) is not soft. It's the trained-to-win posture of a top 1% broker who knows that the next listing comes from the last close · always.
