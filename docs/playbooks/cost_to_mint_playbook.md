## Purpose

This playbook is the operator's guide to communicating cost-to-mint to a client. The math is published in the [Energy and Cost to Mint doctrine](../doctrine/09_energy_and_cost_to_mint.md). This playbook teaches the broker how to walk the client through that math in a way that earns trust · NOT loses it.

The audience for this conversation is almost always the **CFO** (or whatever role serves that function · controller · finance lead · principal's accountant). The CFO is a stakeholder · NOT the buyer. The principal buys the engagement. The CFO underwrites it. Both conversations matter.

---

## The CRE-CFO mindset · what to mirror

A CRE CFO has spent a career reading closing statements. They know the line items by reflex:

- Loan origination
- Title insurance
- Escrow
- Survey
- Environmental
- Lender's appraisal
- Recording fees
- Transfer tax

When you hand them a closing statement · they don't scan it · they REBUILD it. They check the line items against the engagement letter. They verify the math. They flag anything that doesn't tie out.

That's the standard. Match it.

**Mirror this by**:
- Always handing them the 7-component breakdown · NEVER an opaque per-month subscription number
- Always citing the Florida industrial kWh rate as the basis for the energy line (so they can verify it against an external source)
- Always citing the published formula doc URL (so they can re-derive at 2am if they want to)
- Always producing the Hedera HCS anchor for a sample deed (so they can verify the meter against the chain)

If they can't rebuild the math from the artifacts you handed them · you haven't earned it · re-explain.

## The 90-second pitch · for a sit with the CFO

> "We publish the cost-to-mint formula. One Royal Jelly deed on our owned compute costs us $0.0052 all-in. Seven components · all re-derivable · the math is on our /economics page · we update it quarterly with a deed of its own · the deed is Hedera-anchored so you can verify the number didn't change between quarters.
>
> Your fee is the cost-to-mint times a tier multiplier. The multiplier reflects the white-glove level you contract for · pilot is 2x · production is 4x · white-glove is 8x · embedded is 12x. Volume floors are negotiated at LOU signing · everything is in the engagement folder.
>
> The reason we publish this · candidly · is because we want you to audit us the same way you'd audit any other CRE vendor. The formula is the moat. If a competitor can't show you theirs · that's because they don't own their compute."

That's it. 60-90 seconds. Then stop talking and let the CFO ask questions. The questions are how the relationship gets built.

## The 4 questions the CFO will ask · and the right answers

### Q1 · "Why is your white-glove tier 8x your cost? That's a lot of margin."

**Answer**: "It is. The 8x reflects the marginal cost of named human attention. A white-glove engagement has a named sr broker on retainer · a founder reachable on text for material events · a 99.9% SLA · and the right to call us at 02:30 on a Sunday if something goes dark. That's not pure software margin · that's a labor amortization · and our gross margin on that tier is in the 40-50% range · NOT the 90% range a SaaS pitches.

If you don't need the named attention · the production tier at 4x is the same defense apparatus without the dedicated humans · and that's where most of our book lives."

### Q2 · "Can you commit to a fixed monthly?"

**Answer**: "Yes · per assignment · in the LOU. The LOU sets the cost-to-mint ceiling per deed AND a monthly floor (the minimum we run the apparatus for · regardless of deed count). Above the floor · you pay per deed at the contracted rate. We can also do a blended monthly if you prefer · but you'll see the per-deed math in the closing statement either way · so you always know what you actually consumed.

We don't do 'unlimited' tiers. Unlimited is how vendors hide the meter. We're explicit about the meter on purpose."

### Q3 · "What happens if your cost-to-mint goes up?"

**Answer**: "Three things trigger an out-of-cycle update to the formula: GPU power efficiency changes · Hedera mainnet fee schedule changes · validator chain composition changes. When any of those happen · we issue a delta deed explaining what moved · how much · and when. Your contract has a +10% buffer baked into the multiplier · changes inside that buffer don't change your fee. Changes outside that buffer trigger a re-negotiation · in writing · before they hit your invoice. No silent meter changes · ever."

### Q4 · "Can I see the cost-to-mint for our specific engagement · not the published average?"

**Answer**: "Yes. Your Morning Brief carries a 30-day rolling cost-to-mint specific to your fleet. Your closing statement carries the same for the assignment period. If you want a board-prep export · we ship a CSV on request · same data the dashboard surfaces. The dashboard updates daily."

## The 4 mistakes operators make · and how to avoid them

### Mistake 1 · Apologizing for the price

The price is the price. The math is the math. We publish it because we stand behind it. If you sound apologetic · the CFO will negotiate harder · because they smell weakness in the formula.

**Right tone**: "This is what it costs us · this is what we charge · the multiplier reflects the service level · here's the formula."

**Wrong tone**: "I know it might sound high · but..."

### Mistake 2 · Quoting the cost-to-mint as the FEE

Cost-to-mint is the FLOOR. The fee is cost-to-mint times the tier multiplier. If you quote $0.0052/deed as the fee · you've under-priced by 4-12x · and you've trained the CFO to ignore the multiplier.

**Right framing**: "Our cost is $0.0052 · your fee at production tier is $0.0208 · the difference funds the 24/7 ops · the named sr broker · the SLA · the white-glove apparatus."

### Mistake 3 · Hiding the energy line

Energy is 5% of the cost. It's small. But it's the line the next-generation CFO obsesses over. Always surface it · always cite the public rate · always show the kWh-per-deed math.

**Right move**: lead with the energy line when sustainability is on the CFO's mind. The transparency surprises them in a good way.

### Mistake 4 · Negotiating the formula instead of the tier

The published formula is non-negotiable. It's the moat. What's negotiable is:

- Which tier the engagement opens at (T1 pilot → T2 production → T3 white-glove → T4 embedded)
- The monthly floor (volume commitment)
- The volume break above the floor (per-deed price decreases at named thresholds)
- The SLA targets within the tier

If the CFO is negotiating the formula itself · you've explained it wrong · re-explain.

## When the CFO pushes back hard on price

The PASS doctrine applies. Some CFOs want SaaS economics on a broker-grade service. That's a fit mismatch · not a negotiation problem.

**Walk-away script**:

> "We hear you. The economics we offer are calibrated for principals who need a 24/7 defense apparatus they can audit · cite in a regulator filing · and stake the business on. If your use case is lighter-weight · we may not be the right fit · and that's OK. The defendablerouter.com OSS is free and self-serviceable · or there are SaaS competitors who'll quote you a flat monthly that obscures the meter. We'd rather refer you to the right fit than over-promise on ours."

This is the PASS doctrine in action. We don't drop the formula to win the deal. We walk · because the dropped-formula deal becomes the next quarter's loss.

## The closing-statement walk · how to do it in person

When the closing statement lands · the sr broker walks the CFO through it in a 20-30 minute call. The walk has a fixed shape:

1. **The boolean** (CLOSED · HONEY / CONDITIONED / FAILED · RECOVERABLE / FAILED · DARK). One word.
2. **The 5-grade breakdown** · grade by grade · highest to lowest · with one sentence per grade.
3. **The composite** · how the weights produced the number.
4. **The variance vs contract** · per success criterion · plain math.
5. **The cost-to-mint actual vs ceiling** · per-deed average · monthly total · variance.
6. **The deed list** · how many deeds issued · how many Honey · how many Royal Jelly · how many Propolis (and what we did about them).
7. **The Hedera anchor proof** · here's the topic ID · here's the consensus timestamp · here's how to verify.
8. **The next assignment proposal** OR **the graceful exit doc** · in the SAME meeting.

Never email the closing statement and hope they read it. Always walk it. The walk is what earns the next engagement · the next referral · the next 5 years.
