# Probability of Close Playbook

> *"Make the dial. Build the math. Tell the principal where it is. Tell them where it's going. Tell them why."*
>
> — Founder · 2026-05-24

---

## Purpose

This playbook is the operator's guide to communicating the **Probability of Close** dial to a client. The doctrine is in [15_probability_of_close_doctrine.md](../doctrine/15_probability_of_close_doctrine.md). This playbook teaches the broker how to brief it · update it · escalate it · and never lose the principal's trust by spinning it.

The dial is not a marketing number. The dial is the operator's number. When a sr broker shares it with the principal · they share the truth · with the drivers · with the action items · with the timeline. Spin breaks the relationship in one quarter. Honesty earns the next decade.

---

## The 5 bands · and what to say at each

The dial is bucketed into 5 bands for UI clarity and for client communication. Each band has a default script · adjusted for the specific drivers. Use these as starting points · not as rigid scripts.

### GREEN · LOCKED (0.85 - 1.00)

**Default script**:

> "We're locked at [X]. Color is strong across [N] sources · the agent fleet held [Y]% Honey rate last week · zero Propolis events on critical paths. We'll keep the cadence weekly · no surprises expected. If anything moves more than 5 points · I'll call you same-day."

**What NOT to say**:
- "We're definitely closing." (Probability is probability · not certainty.)
- "Nothing can go wrong." (Always something. Don't tempt the dial.)

### AMBER · TRACKING (0.65 - 0.84)

**Default script**:

> "We're at [X] · tracking. Two drivers are off-spec: [driver 1] and [driver 2]. Here's what we're doing on each [name the actions]. I expect us to be back in green by [date]. If we don't get there · I'll re-pencil and you'll see the revised dial in the Friday Morning Brief."

**What NOT to say**:
- "We'll be fine · don't worry about it." (Dismissive · principal feels managed not informed.)
- "It's just a little drop." (Every drop is a signal · respect the signal.)

### YELLOW · WATCHLIST (0.45 - 0.64)

**Default script**:

> "We're at [X] · watchlist. This is serious · not catastrophic. Here's what's underwater: [drivers]. Here's our daily action plan: [actions]. I'm on the desk daily until we move · sr broker review every Friday · and I want to schedule a 30-min principal-call by Thursday to walk you through the remediation options."

**What NOT to say**:
- "We'll know more in a few weeks." (Vague · principal doesn't trust vague.)
- "It's mostly under control." (Either it's under control or it isn't · name it.)

### ORANGE · ESCALATION (0.25 - 0.44)

**Default script**:

> "We're at [X] · escalation. This needs your attention · today. Here's what happened · here's what we're doing · here's the revised flight sheet [share it]. The founder wants 30 minutes with you in the next 48 hours · we have three remediation paths · we'd like your read before we commit. The relationship is fine · the assignment is at risk · we're treating it like a workout."

**What NOT to say**:
- "We can probably save this." (Probably is not a relationship word.)
- "Let me get back to you." (Escalation is now · not later.)

### RED · DARK (0.00 - 0.24)

**Default script**:

> "We're dark. I'm calling because you deserve to hear it from me before the Morning Brief lands. Here's what went wrong · here's the failure deed (pre-staged) · here's our two-path recovery plan · here are the dollar implications under your LOU · the Fix-or-Refund clause is in play if you want it. The founder is available today · I can put him on the line now or schedule for end-of-day · your call."

**What NOT to say**:
- "We're going to turn this around." (Maybe. Don't promise.)
- "These things happen." (They do. But not as the opening line.)

## How to brief the principal · the Morning Brief slot

The dial lives in the Morning Brief (06:00 daily client email). The slot has a fixed 4-element shape:

```
PROBABILITY OF CLOSE · [X.XX] · [BAND NAME]

UP: [What's driving it up · 1 sentence]
DOWN: [What's driving it down · 1 sentence]
ACTION: [One concrete action · with named owner · with date]
```

Examples:

```
PROBABILITY OF CLOSE · 0.91 · GREEN · LOCKED

UP: Honey rate held 94% for the 14th consecutive day · principal completed all diligence items same-day.
DOWN: Nothing material.
ACTION: Continuing weekly cadence · next sr broker review Friday 06:00.
```

```
PROBABILITY OF CLOSE · 0.71 · AMBER · TRACKING

UP: Color file added 2 new EDGAR confirmations · Tribunal pass rate up 3 points week-over-week.
DOWN: Cost-to-mint ran 108% of ceiling last week · one model swap inflated wall time.
ACTION: Sr broker (J. Reyes) re-tuning routing strategy by Wednesday · revised projection in Thursday Morning Brief.
```

The 4-line slot is sacred. Never expand it for marketing language. Never omit it because the dial didn't move. Constancy is the discipline.

## When to escalate to a real call (not just an email)

Email is fine for green and most of amber. The dial gets a voice call when:

| Trigger | Within | Who calls |
|---|---|---|
| Drop > 0.10 in 24 hours | Same day | Sr broker |
| Drop into YELLOW | Same day | Sr broker |
| Drop into ORANGE | 48 hours | Founder + sr broker |
| Drop into RED | Same day | Founder leads |
| Sustained YELLOW for 7+ days with no upward movement | End of week 7 | Founder takes the relationship lead |
| Critical Propolis event regardless of dial | Same day | Sr broker · founder cc |
| Principal asks for a call on the dial | When they want | Sr broker minimum · founder if requested |

The voice call is the white-glove discipline. Email is the operating cadence. Both have their place. Confusing them breaks trust.

## The 6 drivers · how to brief each one

The dial is a 6-driver weighted composite (per the doctrine). Each driver has its own brief shape · its own evidence trail · its own remediation path. When a driver moves the dial · brief the principal on the driver specifically · not the composite.

### Driver 1 · Color strength (25% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Color file at [N] independent sources · contradictions all reconciled · last refresh [date]."

**When it's weak**: "Color file fell to [N] sources after we discounted [source X] for staleness. Re-sourcing in progress · target back to [N+2] by [date]."

### Driver 2 · Principal motivation (20% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Principal answered diligence same-day · prioritized internal stakeholders for our calls · budget confirmed."

**When it's weak**: "Principal has been quieter the past 10 days. Could be travel · could be priority shift · we're checking pulse this week."

### Driver 3 · Asset condition (20% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Honey rate [Y]% · Propolis zero · validator chain pass rate [Z]% · all trending."

**When it's weak**: "Three Propolis events in the past 7 days · all on non-critical paths but we want zero · Tribunal review scheduled."

### Driver 4 · Buyer pool / fit (15% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Our defense capabilities map directly to the principal's workflow · zero capability gaps identified."

**When it's weak**: "One workflow area (regulatory reporting) is at the edge of our current capability · scoping a supplement with the engineering team this week."

### Driver 5 · Comp quality (10% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Three comparable engagements (similar industry · similar agent fleet size) closed HONEY in the past 6 months · giving us high confidence in the dial."

**When it's weak**: "This engagement is unusual in [way] · we have fewer than 2 close comps · widening the confidence interval on the dial."

### Driver 6 · Operator hygiene (10% weight)

**When it's strong**: "Cost-to-mint ran 96% of ceiling · all deeds anchored on time · Morning Briefs hit 06:00 every day."

**When it's weak**: "Cost-to-mint at 109% last week · one missed anchor batch (recovered by next cron) · we're tightening."

## What to do when the principal disagrees with the dial

It happens. The principal sees their internal data · believes the deal is stronger or weaker than the dial reports. The sr broker's job is to walk the math · NOT to defend the dial.

**Right move**:

> "Help me understand what you're seeing. Walk me through your view. I want to test our drivers against your read · because if there's a delta · either our drivers are missing something or your view has signal we haven't captured. Either way · we should know."

Then LISTEN. Take notes. Take the disagreement back to the Tribunal as a calibration signal. The next dial update reflects what you learned · with the principal's input cited.

**Wrong move**:

> "The dial is what the dial is."

That sentence ends relationships. The dial is a model · models have error bars · principals have signal · the broker integrates both.

---

## Annual calibration

Once a year · the engagement runs a **calibration report** comparing the dial's predictions to actual outcomes across all closed assignments. The report ships as a deed (`DDEED-{org}-CALIB-{year}-v1`). The principal gets a copy. The math is published.

If the dial systematically over-predicts (too optimistic) · the formula gets revised. If it under-predicts (too conservative) · same. The calibration is the credibility loop · without it the dial is theater.
